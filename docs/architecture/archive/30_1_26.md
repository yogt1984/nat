## Summary of Project

The user is developing **NAT (Entropy-Based Regime Detection Agent Swarm)** - a sophisticated algorithmic trading system designed to detect market regimes and dynamically select optimal trading strategies based on entropy and information-theoretic analysis for the Hyperliquid decentralized perpetual futures exchange.

---

## Initial Project Assessment

### What The Project Aims To Do

1. **Detect market regimes** using entropy measures (permutation entropy, Shannon entropy, sample entropy)
2. **Route strategies** dynamically - executing mean-reversion (Avellaneda-Stoikov) or trend-following strategies based on detected regime
3. **Evolve parameters** through genetic algorithms to optimize performance across different market conditions
4. **Apply novel mathematics** including information geometry, transfer entropy networks, and polynomial chaos expansion

### Core Objective

Solve a fundamental problem in quantitative trading: **strategy selection is regime-dependent**. Mean-reversion strategies work in stable/ranging markets, while trend-following strategies work in directional/volatile markets. Rather than guessing which regime the market is in, the goal is to **detect it empirically using information-theoretic measures**.

### Scientific Approach

The methodology is built on three key principles:

1. **Entropy as a regime signal** — Using permutation entropy, Shannon entropy, and sample entropy to quantify market disorder. Low entropy indicates predictable/mean-reverting conditions; high entropy suggests trending/chaotic conditions.

2. **Information geometry** — Instead of treating features as Euclidean vectors, modeling market states on a Fisher information manifold where statistical distances (geodesics) better capture regime boundaries.

3. **Unsupervised regime discovery** — Rather than defining regimes circularly by which strategy is profitable (tautological), first discovering natural market clusters on the information manifold, then empirically determining what works in each state.

## Fisher Information Manifold Explanation

### What Is Fisher Information?

Fisher information measures **how much information a random variable X carries about an unknown parameter θ**.

**Formal definition** (single parameter):
```
I(θ) = E[ (∂/∂θ log p(X|θ))² ]
     = -E[ ∂²/∂θ² log p(X|θ) ]
```

### The Fisher Information Manifold

Probability distributions can be viewed as points on a manifold. The Fisher Information Matrix defines a **Riemannian metric** on this manifold.

This metric tells you:
- How to measure **distances** between nearby distributions
- How to measure **angles** between directions in parameter space
- The **curvature** of the space at each point

### Application to Market States

At each time t, the market state is characterized by a **distribution** over features. Each distribution is a **point on the Fisher manifold**.

As market evolves, it traces a PATH on this manifold. Regime = REGION on manifold. Regime change = Path crosses region boundary.

### Advantages

| Concept | Euclidean View | Fisher Manifold View |
|---------|----------------|---------------------|
| **Distance between states** | Arbitrary (depends on scaling) | Statistically meaningful |
| **Regime boundary** | Hyperplane (arbitrary) | Geodesic boundary (natural) |
| **Interpolation** | Linear (may not be valid distribution) | Geodesic (always valid distribution) |
| **Curvature** | None | Indicates complexity/uncertainty |

## Momentum Strategy Assessment

### The Evidence (Traditional Markets)

Momentum is one of the most robust anomalies ever documented in traditional markets (Jegadeesh & Titman 1993, Moskowitz et al. 2012, Hurst et al. 2017).

### When Momentum Fails

- **Momentum crashes** — Sharp reversals cause severe drawdowns
- **Choppy/ranging markets** — No trend to follow
- **High transaction costs** — Alpha erodes
- **Crowding** — Signal arbitraged away
- **Regime transitions** — Worst performance at turning points

### Momentum in Crypto

Evidence is mixed:
- **Intraday (minutes)**: Mean-reverting
- **Short-term (hours-days)**: Weak momentum, high noise
- **Medium-term (weeks)**: Some momentum, inconsistent
- **Long-term (months)**: Strong momentum during bull markets, crashes during reversals

### Recommendation

Pure momentum following is **not recommended**. Better: Momentum + regime filter, momentum + volatility scaling, momentum + mean reversion ensemble.

## Liquidity and Price Movement

### The Claim: "Price Moves Toward Liquidity"

**Assessment**: Partially true but often misunderstood.

### What's Actually Happening

- **Liquidity consumption**: Price moves BY consuming liquidity, not TO liquidity
- **Stop cascades**: When stops trigger, they become market orders → push price further → trigger more stops
- **Liquidation mechanics**: In leveraged markets, liquidation levels are known, crossing them triggers forced orders

### More Accurate Mental Model

Price doesn't "seek" liquidity — it **accelerates through low-liquidity zones** and **slows in high-liquidity zones**.

### TRENDING MARKET Expectations

| **ent_permutation_returns_*** | **LOW (0.2-0.5)** | Returns have consistent sign; ordinal patterns dominated by monotonic sequences |

| **flow_aggressor_ratio** | **BIASED (>0.6 or <0.4)** | Buy aggressors dominate in uptrend |
| **vol_returns_1m/5m** | **ELEVATED** | Directional moves have higher volatility |

### MEAN-REVERTING / RANGING MARKET Expectations

| Feature | Expected Behavior | Why |
|---------|-------------------|-----|
| **ent_permutation_returns_*** | **HIGH (0.7-0.95)** | Returns oscillate randomly; all ordinal patterns appear |
| **imbalance_qty_l1/l5/l10** | **OSCILLATING around 0** | Alternates between positive and negative |
| **flow_aggressor_ratio** | **CENTERED (~0.5)** | Buy and sell aggression balanced |
| **vol_returns_1m/5m** | **LOW** | Small price movements, compressed range |

### Critical Transitions

**End of Bull Run (before reversal):**
- Entropy RISING
- Imbalance STILL POSITIVE but weakening
- Aggressor ratio DECLINING
- Pressure_bid DECLINING, pressure_ask INCREASING

**Trend Initiation (at breakout):**
- Entropy DROPPING rapidly
- Imbalance SWINGING to extreme
- Aggressor ratio SWINGING to extreme
- Volume/intensity SPIKING

### 1. Entropy Dynamics Features (6 features)

### 2. Imbalance Persistence Features (7 features)

- `imb_persistence_30s`: time with same sign / 30s
- `imb_directional_strength`: Σimb / Σ|imb|

- `imb_is_weakening`: sign(imb) ≠ sign(imb_velocity)
- `imb_cumulative_30s`: Σimb over 30s

### 4. Composite Scores (5 features)
