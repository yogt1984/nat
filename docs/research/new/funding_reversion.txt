1. THESIS
================================================================================

Perpetual futures have no expiry date. To keep the perpetual price anchored
to spot, exchanges use a funding rate mechanism: every 8 hours, one side of
the market pays the other.

  - Perp > Spot (positive premium): longs pay shorts
  - Perp < Spot (negative premium): shorts pay longs

When the funding rate becomes extreme, it reveals that the market is heavily
positioned in one direction and paying dearly to maintain it. These crowded
positions tend to unwind, causing price to revert.

The algorithm enters opposite to the extreme: when funding is extremely
positive (crowd is long, paying to stay long), go short — the crowd will
eventually capitulate. When funding is extremely negative (crowd is short),
go long.

This signal is crypto-native. Traditional futures use physical delivery at
expiry to anchor basis; perpetual futures use the funding rate instead. The
funding rate essentially reveals the cost of the consensus trade — when that
cost becomes extreme, the consensus is about to break.

================================================================================
2. SIGNAL CONSTRUCTION
================================================================================

Inputs (from Rust ingestor, ctx_* features):
  - ctx_funding_rate      Current 8h funding rate (annualized)
  - ctx_funding_zscore    Z-score of funding rate vs rolling history
  - ctx_premium_bps       Spot-perpetual premium in basis points

2.1  Reversion signal (primary)
---------------------------------------------------------------------

  if |z| >= z_entry:
      signal = -sign(z) * min(|z| / z_entry, 3.0) / 3.0
  else:
      signal = 0.0

  where z = ctx_funding_zscore, z_entry = 2.0 (default)

  Output range: [-1.0, +1.0]

  Examples:
    z = +3.0  ->  signal = -sign(3) * min(3/2, 3) / 3 = -1.0 * 1.5/3 = -0.50
    z = +6.0  ->  signal = -sign(6) * min(6/2, 3) / 3 = -1.0 * 3.0/3 = -1.00
    z = -4.0  ->  signal = -sign(-4) * min(4/2, 3) / 3 = +1.0 * 2.0/3 = +0.67
    z = +1.5  ->  signal = 0.0  (below threshold, no trade)

  Key properties:
    - The -sign(z) is the contrarian core: go OPPOSITE to the crowd
    - Linear scaling between z_entry and 3*z_entry (conviction grows with
      extremity, capped to prevent overcommitting in tail events)
    - Dead zone below z_entry: normal funding is not tradeable
    - The 3.0 cap means max conviction requires funding to be 6 standard
      deviations from normal — truly extreme positioning

2.2  Funding momentum (trend detection)
---------------------------------------------------------------------

  ema_funding(t) = alpha * rate(t) + (1 - alpha) * ema_funding(t-1)

  where alpha = 2 / (momentum_span + 1), momentum_span = 100

  This tracks the funding rate trend. Rising EMA = funding becoming more
  positive = crowd accumulating longs. The momentum itself is not used in
  the primary signal but is exposed as alg_funding_momentum for downstream
  combination or analysis.

2.3  Premium divergence
---------------------------------------------------------------------

  premium_z = ctx_premium_bps / 10.0    (rough normalization)
  divergence = 0.7 * zscore + 0.3 * premium_z

  Combines funding rate with spot-perp premium:
    - Agreement (both positive): crowd is unambiguously long, strong signal
    - Divergence (funding positive, premium negative): unusual state,
      weaker signal — something structural may be happening

  Exposed as alg_premium_divergence for downstream use.

2.4  OU half-life (reversion speed estimation)
---------------------------------------------------------------------

  From a rolling buffer of ctx_funding_zscore (200 ticks):

    rho = lag-1 autocorrelation of z-score
    half_life = -ln(2) / ln(rho)

  Interpretation:
    rho in (0, 1):  mean-reverting, half_life = ticks to revert halfway
    rho <= 0:       anti-persistent, instant reversion (half_life = 0)
    rho >= 1:       trending / random walk, no reversion (half_life = inf)

  This tells us whether the fundamental assumption (funding mean-reverts)
  holds right now. If half_life is very long, the reversion trade may not
  work in the current regime.

  Exposed as alg_funding_halflife_ticks for regime filtering.

================================================================================
5. WHY IT IS CRYPTO-NATIVE
================================================================================

In traditional finance:
  - Futures expire. The basis between futures and spot converges mechanically
    at expiry via physical or cash delivery.
  - There is no funding rate. The basis is the cost of carry (interest +
    storage - convenience yield).
  - Position crowding is visible only through COT reports (weekly, delayed).

In crypto perpetual futures:
  - No expiry, no delivery. The funding rate IS the anchoring mechanism.
  - Funding rate is public, real-time, and directly reveals crowd positioning.
  - Extreme funding = crowd paying to hold a directional bet = mean-reversion
    opportunity.
  - 8-hour funding cycles create periodic forced cash flows that pressure
    overleveraged positions to unwind.

No traditional finance algorithm exploits this signal because the mechanism
does not exist in traditional markets.

================================================================================
8. RESEARCH DIRECTIONS
================================================================================

8.1  Half-life gating
  Only trade when alg_funding_halflife_ticks < threshold (e.g., < 100 ticks).
  This would filter out periods where funding is trending rather than
  mean-reverting, improving hit rate at the cost of fewer trades.

8.2  Asymmetric entry
  Funding reversion may be asymmetric: shorts capitulate faster than longs
  (liquidation cascades are more violent for shorts in crypto because of
  the long-bias of the market). Test separate z_entry thresholds for
  positive vs negative funding.

8.3  Funding rate prediction
  Rather than trading the current z-score, predict the NEXT funding rate
  change using order flow features. If microstructure signals (hawkes,
  OFI) can predict funding rate changes before they happen, the entry
  can be earlier and more profitable.

8.4  Cross-symbol funding arbitrage
  When BTC funding is extremely positive but ETH funding is neutral,
  it may signal BTC-specific crowding. The cross-symbol funding spread
  (BTC funding - ETH funding) could be a standalone signal.

8.5  8-hour cycle exploitation
  Funding payments occur every 8 hours (00:00, 08:00, 16:00 UTC on
  Hyperliquid). Position unwinds tend to cluster in the 1-2 hours before
  funding payment as leveraged traders reduce exposure. Time-of-day
  conditioning could improve the signal.
